janenlies. tuin en landschap.

Is a team of garden designers with a vision to support a lifetime.

From the mathematical elegance of Fibonacci patterns to the clever design of leaves—their crystalline surfaces creating a natural self-cleaning system—Janenlies discover inspiration in nature's perfect engineering.

Breaking from a norm, they think in generations, not seasons. Seeing gardens as living stories unfolding over decades, embracing nature's wisdom that balance comes through diversity and that decay always creates space for renewal.

Janenlies designs spaces where every element has both purpose and meaning. Their work is about thoughtful ecosystems that evolve and transform. Designing with nature's intelligence, creating landscapes that grow more beautiful and complex over time.

Their vision brings harmony back to our surroundings—not by imposing order, but by revealing the inherent balance that exists when we listen to what the natural world has been teaching us all along.

info

The studio began with two architects who saw gardens as living art, and now continues through their children. For generations, this family has created landscapes that blend architectural precision with botanical poetry.

The approach selects materials that age gracefully and arranges plants as characters in a story. Through patient observation and creative imagination, making spaces where people rediscover a connection to the natural world.

The studio is currently engaging with select new projects. For conversations about their distinct temporal approach to landscape architecture or to explore their portfolio of ecological narratives, reach out directly.

Growth and nature are a obvious but all-encompassing combination. As garden architects, Jan en Lies do not confine themselves to the present moment, but always think long-term. They research, adapt to, and make use of the beauty in the qualities and growth found in the green world around us. When a tree grows, its branches extend and widen, the tree becomes taller and its trunk thicker. When our children grow they become tallerand stronger. When a river grows its boundaries swell.

Everything in nature grows within a finite space, and balance exists when diversity is high and each species of plant, animal, insect and microbe fill a certain role within their given boundaries.

If we turn to the wisdom of our elders and the metaphors of nature, we learn that growth is followed by decay, a dying off, and rebirth again. We learn that each time something dies, it creates a space for something new to be created in its place. We learn that balance occurs when there is diversity of life, and that innovation takes place in a climate of harmony. It’s harmony that is key. It’s balance that we want to bring back all around us. And it’s growth that we keep in mind for the future of our gardens.

We like to believe that everything has a purpose, not just its physical role in the eco system, but a deeper meaning and reason for being.

That’s why ‘coming up soon’ does not remind us of a new and temporary collections of ‘anythings’, but rather of what is growing out of our ideas and ecosystems.
